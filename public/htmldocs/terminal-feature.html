📰 Smart News Engine AI Filtered
Fetches 100+ articles → filters down to 80 relevant, ranked by impact + freshness
How We Fetch News (2 Sources)
  • Google News RSS — 6 search-based feeds
  • Economic Times RSS — 2 direct feeds (markets + stocks)
  • Each feed returns up to 20 items → parsed with custom XML regex parser
  • All 8 feeds fetched in parallel (Promise.allSettled)
  • 60s server-side cache (Next.js revalidate)
Why Google News = All Sources
  • Google News is an aggregator — it pulls from all Indian publishers
  • Each article has a <source> tag with original publisher name
  • We extract it → UI shows "Moneycontrol", "CNBC TV18", "LiveMint", "Business Standard", "ET" etc.
  • So we get all major sources in one RSS call — no per-source API needed
6 Google News Search Feeds
  • NSE BSE stock market india
  • nifty sensex today
  • india stock earnings results quarterly
  • RBI SEBI india economy policy
  • india IPO bulk deal block deal buyback
  • india merger acquisition order win contract
Relevance Scoring
  • 22 high-impact patterns with weights (7–10)
  • Bulk/block deals, insider buying → weight 10
  • Quarterly results, beat/miss estimates → weight 9–10
  • RBI rate decisions, SEBI orders → weight 9–10
  • Order wins, M&A, fundraising → weight 8–9
  • Noise filter — "stocks to watch", "how to invest" → downranked −5
Freshness Decay
  • <1h old → +10 pts
  • 1–2h → +8 pts
  • 2–4h → +6 pts
  • 4–8h → +4 pts
  • 8–24h → +2 pts
  • >24h → dropped entirely
Sentiment Analysis
  • Each article tagged: Bullish Bearish Neutral
  • Scans headline + description for keyword frequency
  • Bullish words (14): surge, rally, gain, jump, soar, beat, upgrade, wins, approved, bags…
  • Bearish words (16): fall, crash, plunge, miss, downgrade, penalty, ban, probe, default…
  • bull_count > bear_count → Bullish (and vice versa)
  • Feeds into AI Market Pulse on dashboard
🚫 Noise Filter — What Gets Downranked (−5 pts)
  • "expert says", "analyst says", "market expert"
  • "stocks to watch", "top picks", "best stocks"
  • "5 things to know", "10 things", "things to know"
  • "how to invest", "how to trade", "investment tips"
  • "here's why" clickbait articles

These articles are opinion/listicle type — not actual market-moving news. They appear at the bottom of the feed.

✅ Final Ranking Formula

Total Score = Impact Score + Freshness Score + Stock Match Bonus
Impact Score: sum of matched keyword weights (7–10 each)
Freshness: 0–10 pts based on article age
Stock Match: +2 pts if article mentions a tracked NSE stock
Noise penalty: −5 pts

Sorted: non-noise first, then by score. Deduplication by first 60 chars of title. Top 80 returned.

1
Article appears in Google News RSS — the feed india IPO bulk deal block deal buyback picks up a new article published 30 minutes ago:
Title: "HDFC Bank sees bulk deal worth ₹500 Cr, promoter increases stake"
Source tag: <source>Moneycontrol</source>
2
XML parsing — our custom regex parser extracts: title, link, description, pubDate, and sourceName = "Moneycontrol". The display source is set to "Moneycontrol" (not "Google News").
3
Impact scoring — full text "HDFC Bank sees bulk deal worth ₹500 Cr, promoter increases stake" is scanned against 22 patterns:
· Pattern /bulk deal|block deal/i → matches "bulk deal" → +10 pts
· Pattern /insider buy|promoter buy|promoter increas/i → matches "promoter increases" → +10 pts
· No other patterns match. Impact score = 20 → capped: Impact = HIGH, Timing = immediate
4
Noise check — none of the noise patterns match ("expert says", "stocks to watch" etc.). isNoise = false. No penalty.
5
Freshness — article was published 30 min ago. Age < 1h → +10 pts
6
Sentiment — scans for bullish/bearish words:
· "increases" → not in list. "stake" → not in list. But "bulk deal" is associated with buying → let's check actual words...
· Title contains no explicit bearish words. Contains implicit positive context. Sentiment = Neutral (no keyword exact match). The article overall is positive but our simple keyword check doesn't find a direct match here.
7
Related stocks — full text is scanned against STOCK_KEYWORDS. "hdfc bank" matches → HDFCBANK added. relatedStocks = ["HDFCBANK"]. Stock match bonus: +2 pts
8
Final total score = Impact (20) + Freshness (10) + Stock Match (2) = 32 pts
✅ Result: This article scores 32 pts — it will appear near the TOP of the news feed, tagged as HIGH impact, IMMEDIATE timing, with HDFCBANK chip shown, source shown as "Moneycontrol". A user opens the app 30 min after the news breaks and sees this at position #1 or #2 — ready to act before most retail traders even notice.
Contrast — Low score example: "Top 5 stocks to watch tomorrow — expert says Nifty may rally" → matches noise pattern "stocks to watch" + "expert says" → isNoise = true → −5 penalty. Impact patterns: none. Freshness: say 3h old → +6. Score = 0 + 6 + 0 − 5 = 1 pt. This gets pushed to the bottom of the feed.

🔍 Trade Finder 5 Strategies
Scans 30 NSE stocks post-market (4:30 PM IST) daily. Results stored in Supabase → shown next morning. Signals are for the next trading day.
⚠️ Important: How to Read Bullish / Bearish Tags

Bullish tag = Pattern suggests the stock will likely move UP next day → potential BUY / LONG entry for intraday or swing
Bearish tag = Pattern suggests the stock will likely move DOWN next day → potential SELL / SHORT entry (or avoid buying)

These are pattern-based signals, not guaranteed predictions. Always cross-check with ML prediction badge (shows confidence %) and current news before acting. Use stop-loss.

S1 · STRICT MORNING TREND  Score: 92/100 — Most Reliable

Exact Logic (from code)

  • Looks at 9:30–10:30 AM window (5-min candles)
  • Every single candle must close higher than previous (bullish) OR lower than previous (bearish)
  • This must be true on all 3 of the last 3 trading days
  • No exceptions — if even one candle breaks the chain, it's rejected
  • Minimum 4 candles needed per day to qualify

How to Act on Signal

  • Bullish → Next day, watch 9:30–10:00 AM. If stock starts moving up → enter BUY around 9:35–9:45 AM. Target: 10:30 AM high. Exit before 3:15 PM.
  • Bearish → Next day, if stock starts dropping at open → short entry or avoid buying. Momentum likely continues down in morning session.
  • Best for: Intraday morning trades (9:30–11:00 AM window)
  • Why reliable: 3 consecutive days of perfectly continuous trend = very strong momentum pattern
S2 · GENERAL MORNING TREND  Score: 85/100

Exact Logic (from code)

  • Morning window: 9:30–10:30 AM (first candle open → last candle close)
  • Afternoon window: 10:30 AM–3:30 PM (first candle open → last candle close)
  • Morning direction must match afternoon direction on that day
  • Afternoon move must be ≥100 pts (both bullish and bearish)
  • All 3 of the last 3 days must be consistent in the same direction

How to Act on Signal

  • Bullish → Morning sets tone. Watch first 15 min after 9:30. If bullish → enter BUY. Hold till 1–2 PM (afternoon follow-through expected). Good for full-day intraday.
  • Bearish → Morning drop tends to continue all day. If stock opens weak or drops early → short or don't buy. Afternoon likely stays negative.
  • ≥100 pts filter ensures the move is meaningful, not just noise
  • Best for: Full-day intraday or short swing (1–2 days)
S3 · CANDLE PATTERN DETECTION  Score: 75–80/100

4 Patterns Detected (5-min candles)

  • Hammer Bullish — Long lower wick ≥2× body + small upper wick ≤0.5× body. Buyers rejected the low strongly. Reversal signal.
  • Shooting Star Bearish — Long upper wick ≥2× body + small lower wick. Price tried to rally, sellers pushed it back. Reversal signal.
  • Strong Bullish Candle Bullish — Close near high, >0.8% move up, tiny upper wick ≤30% of body. Sustained buying with no rejection.
  • Strong Bearish Candle Bearish — Close near low, >0.8% drop, tiny lower wick ≤30% of body. Sustained selling with no bounce.

How to Act on Signal

  • Hammer → Next day, if stock opens flat/down → BUY near open. Reversal from low expected. Place SL below hammer's low.
  • Shooting Star → Next day, if stock opens flat/up → consider SHORT near open or avoid buying. Reversal from high expected.
  • Strong Bullish → Continuation signal. Buy on slight dip from open. Target: yesterday's high. Quick intraday.
  • Strong Bearish → Continuation DOWN. Short on bounce or avoid. Target: yesterday's low.
  • Difference: Hammer/Shooting Star = reversal patterns. Strong Bull/Bear = continuation patterns.
S4 · LONG TREND (≥1 Month)  Score: 65–95/100 (scales with duration)

Exact Logic (from code)

  • Bullish: Price > 20-day SMA > 50-day SMA for ≥20 consecutive trading days
  • Bearish: Price < 20-day SMA < 50-day SMA for ≥20 consecutive days
  • Requires 60-day daily candle history
  • Overextension filter: If price is >15% away from 20MA → rejected (too risky to chase)
  • Score increases with trend duration: 65 (20 days) → up to 95 (2+ months)
  • Entry quality shown: "Excellent" (<5% from 20MA), "Acceptable" (5–10%), "Wait for pullback" (>10%)

How to Act on Signal

  • Bullish → This is a swing/positional trade, not intraday. Entry near 20MA on any dip. Hold 3–10 days. Target: recent highs. SL: below 20MA.
  • Bearish → Stock in confirmed downtrend. Avoid buying. Short on bounces. Or hold positions in short direction.
  • "Excellent entry zone" means price is near 20MA right now — ideal low-risk entry point
  • "Wait for pullback" means stock is extended — entering now = chasing, higher risk of reversal
  • Best for: 3–15 day swing trades, not intraday
S5 · HIGH VOLATILITY MORNING MOVE  Score: 60–95/100

Exact Logic (from code)

  • Formula: ((High − Low) / Open) × 100 in 9:30–10:30 AM window
  • Must be ≥2.2% (qualifies as volatile) on all 3 recent days
  • Must be <4.5% (overextension guard — too wild = reversal risk)
  • At least 2 of 3 days need morning volume ≥1.5× daily average volume → adds +7 score
  • Direction = based on today's overall change% (positive = bullish, negative = bearish)

How to Act on Signal

  • This signal means the stock has been consistently active in first hour for 3 days straight
  • Bullish → Wait for stock to start moving at open (9:30–10:00). Enter after 10:05 AM once direction is confirmed. Ride the volatile move. Exit before 3:15 PM.
  • Bearish → Short after 10:05 AM on confirmation. Same exit rule — before 3:15 PM.
  • "Vol ✓" badge = volume confirmed → stronger signal, higher conviction
  • Best for: Pure intraday scalping. Not for overnight holds.
  • Risk: High volatility = high reward but also quick reversals. Tight SL required.

🤖 ML Predictions Nightly Model
Next-day direction prediction per stock using Logistic Regression — runs nightly at 8 PM IST after the trade scan
Model: Logistic Regression
  • Library: scikit-learn — LogisticRegression
  • Type: Binary classifier — bullish (1) vs bearish (0)
  • Config: random_state=42, max_iter=1000, C=1.0
  • Why Logistic Regression? — simple, fast, interpretable, works well for binary outcome with small dataset (30 days history)
  • Features normalized with StandardScaler before training
  • Validation: 5-fold cross-validation (falls back to 2–5 splits if data is small)
4 Input Features
FeatureWhat it is
scoreSignal strength (0–100) from trade scan
change_percentPrevious day's price change %
direction_encBullish=1, Bearish=0 from strategy
strategy_encStrategy weight: S1=5, S2=4, S3=3, S4=2, S5=1

Strategy weight reflects reliability: S1 (most reliable) = 5, S5 (most volatile) = 1

Training & Inference Flow
  • Fetch 30 days of historical signals from trade_finder_results
  • Minimum 20 rows required — skip if not enough data
  • Label = next-day actual price change > 0 → bullish, else bearish
  • Split: rows with known next-day outcome = train set; today's signals = predict set
  • Apply StandardScaler → train LogisticRegression → predict_proba()
  • Confidence = max(probability) × 100 (e.g. 73% confident bullish)
Grading & Accuracy
  • Next night: compare yesterday's predictions vs actual market outcomes
  • Updates was_correct (boolean) + actual_direction per prediction row
  • Accuracy = correct_predictions / total × 100, stored in ml_daily_accuracy
  • ≥65% → Strong ● — use confidently
  • 55–64% → Moderate ● — use with caution
  • <55% → Weak ● — supplementary only
  • Last 14 days + 7-day rolling avg shown on accuracy page
GitHub Actions Workflow (ml-predict.yml)
  • Runs at 8:00 PM IST Mon–Fri (after 4:30 PM scan + 8 PM ML)
  • Command: python scripts/predict_trend.py
  • Email sent with: predictions saved count, training rows, model accuracy
  • Dependencies: scikit-learn 1.5.0, pandas 2.2.2, numpy 1.26.4, supabase 2.10.0
How It Appears in UI
  • Each trade signal in Trade Finder shows an ML badge
  • Badge shows: direction (↑ Bullish / ↓ Bearish) + confidence % (e.g. 73%)
  • ML badge = secondary confirmation, not primary signal
  • Cross-check: if S1 says Bullish AND ML says Bullish 78% → higher conviction
  • If strategy says Bullish but ML says Bearish 65% → proceed cautiously
Interview answer: "We use a Logistic Regression binary classifier from scikit-learn. It trains nightly on 30 days of historical trade signals. The 4 input features are: signal score (0–100), previous day change %, strategy direction encoded as 0 or 1, and strategy type encoded as a weight (1–5 based on reliability). We apply StandardScaler, run 5-fold cross-validation, and use predict_proba() to get a confidence score. The next night, we grade predictions against actual outcomes and track daily accuracy. ≥65% accuracy is our threshold for calling the model reliable. The ML badge appears next to each trade signal as a second opinion, not the primary recommendation."

💬 Quick Interview Answers
Q: What is DT's Terminal?

A free stock market scanner for Indian traders. It shows live NSE/BSE data, filters market news using AI relevance scoring, runs 5 technical strategies daily on 30 stocks, and provides ML-based next-day predictions — all in one dashboard.

Q: How does the Smart News Engine work? What sources?

We use Google News RSS as our primary source — it's an aggregator that pulls from Moneycontrol, LiveMint, CNBC TV18, Business Standard, Economic Times, and more. Each article in the RSS feed has a <source> tag with the original publisher name, so the UI shows "Moneycontrol" or "CNBC TV18" even though we're fetching from a single Google News feed. We run 6 topic-specific Google News feeds + 2 direct Economic Times feeds, all in parallel. Each article is scored with 22 impact-weighted regex patterns (bulk deals, results, RBI actions), freshness decay, and sentiment (14 bullish / 16 bearish keywords). Articles older than 24h are dropped. Top 80 returned.

Q: Can you walk through how a specific news article gets scored?

Sure. Say HDFC Bank has a bulk deal and Moneycontrol publishes "HDFC Bank sees bulk deal worth ₹500 Cr, promoter increases stake" 30 minutes ago. The article appears in our Google News "bulk deal" feed. We parse the RSS XML, extract the <source> tag = "Moneycontrol". Then we scan the text against 22 patterns: "bulk deal" matches (weight 10), "promoter increases" matches (weight 10), total impact = 20. No noise patterns match so no penalty. Freshness: 30 min old = +10 pts. Related stock: "hdfc bank" maps to HDFCBANK symbol = +2 pts. Final score = 32 pts. This article appears at the top of the feed, tagged HIGH impact, with HDFCBANK chip shown and "Moneycontrol" as source — all within 30 seconds of the next news refresh.

Q: What API do you use for real-time market data?

Yahoo Finance public API — query1.finance.yahoo.com/v8/finance/chart. It's free, no API key required. We fetch OHLCV data for indices and stocks. Dashboard polls it every 60 seconds. For the Trade Finder, we fetch 60-day daily + 10-day 5-min candles per stock during the cron job.

Q: How does the daily cron scan work?

GitHub Actions triggers a cron at 4:30 PM IST every weekday. It runs a TypeScript script that fetches candle data for 30 stocks, applies 5 strategy algorithms, scores each result, and stores everything in Supabase. Before running, it checks if the scan was already done today (idempotency). After running, if 0 stocks were processed, it means it was a market holiday — a skip email is sent instead of a completed email.

Q: What ML model do you use and how does it work?

Logistic Regression from scikit-learn — binary classifier predicting bullish (1) or bearish (0) for next day. It trains nightly on 30 days of historical trade signals with 4 features: signal score (0–100), previous day change %, direction encoded as 0/1, and strategy type as a reliability weight (S1=5 most reliable, S5=1 least). StandardScaler normalizes features, 5-fold cross-validation validates the model, then predict_proba() gives a confidence score like 73%. The next night, we grade yesterday's predictions against actual outcomes and track accuracy. ≥65% accuracy = reliable. The ML badge in Trade Finder shows direction + confidence as a second opinion alongside the strategy signal.

Q: Why Logistic Regression and not a neural network or random forest?

We only have 30 days × 30 stocks = ~900 rows maximum. Neural networks and random forests need far more data to avoid overfitting. Logistic Regression is appropriate for small, clean, tabular datasets with binary outcomes. It's also fast to retrain every night (milliseconds), interpretable, and gives well-calibrated probability scores. As the dataset grows over months, we can evaluate switching to gradient boosting or random forest.
